About The City Food Fun Fact

Although, technically a town, Manali is easily up there as one of the best places to visit in India. Manali boasts some absolutely stunning cascading waterfalls along with rather lush plants and interesting wildlife. Manali is one of those places in India that will totally take you by surprise and challenge your pre-conceived notion of India starting with snow-capped mountains (the Himalayas are here you see), green valleys, and towering waterfalls. You can even go skiing here.

Manali food largely owes itself to the crops that are widely grown in the surrounding valley. The main crops are paddy, maize, kodra and salyara that make up the main dishes of the Manali people. A large number of vegetables and fruits also dominate food of Manali. Vada, bhatora and patrodu are served during celebrations and festivities.

Rich heritage

Legend has it that Manali has been named after Manu, the legendary figure of the Hindu ancient scriptures, who is said to be the progenitor of the human race. It is also believed that the Saptarishis (seven celestial sages) had lived here.

Hanuman’s mother meditated here

We’ve heard of a large snow-made Shivling of Amarnath. But, there is one in Manali’s snow-capped peaks too! As per a local legend, Lord Hanuman’s mother Anjani had meditated and worshipped Lord Shiva where the Shivaling exists today.

A mini-Israel exists near Manali

Kasol is a small town near Manali where you can find cafés and crowds of Israelis. Even the food menu is all in Hebrew. Here, people greet each other with a Shalom instead of a Namaste, and Israeli flags can be seen at different points.

A ghost temple on Gata Loops

On the Manali-Leh road at a height of about 17000 ft above sea level are 21 sharp hair pin bend-like roads called Gata Loops. At one such road is located a ghost temple dedicated to a person who died of hunger and thirst about three decades ago while stranded in a truck all alone. In order to pacify the spirit of the man, every person has to offer a bottle of water and a packet of cigarettes or face severe consequences.

India’s highest sky bicycle track
The highest sky bicycle track India has been constructed at an altitude of 9000 feet at Gulaba.
